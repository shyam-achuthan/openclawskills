# Google Scholar Research Rules

## Query Construction
- Exact phrases in quotes: "machine learning" finds the phrase, not separate words
- Exclude terms with minus: neural networks -deep removes deep learning results
- OR for alternatives: "global warming" OR "climate change" â€” must be uppercase
- author: operator for specific researchers: author:"Y LeCun"
- source: for specific journals: source:"Nature" quantum computing
- intitle: forces term in title: intitle:transformer attention

## Time Filters
- Custom range critical for recent research â€” default shows highly cited old papers first
- "Since 2023" for cutting-edge but less vetted work
- "Since 2020" balances recency and citation accumulation
- Sort by date for newest, sort by relevance for most cited â€” toggle based on need

## Understanding Results
- Citation count indicates influence, not quality â€” popular isn't always right
- "Cited by X" link shows who built on this work â€” follow research forward
- "Related articles" finds similar work â€” useful after finding one good paper
- Versions link shows preprints and alternate copies â€” often free when journal isn't
- Quotation marks in snippet show exact matches â€” verify relevance before clicking

## Finding Full Text
- Check "All versions" for free copies â€” preprints, institutional repositories
- Add filetype:pdf to find direct PDF links
- Unpaywall browser extension auto-finds legal free versions
- Request from authors directly â€” most will share, academia.edu and ResearchGate common
- Institutional access through library proxy â€” VPN to university network

## Citation Analysis
- h-index visible on author profiles â€” measures productivity and impact
- "Cited by" sorted by relevance shows most influential citations
- Citation count alone misleads â€” review papers cite heavily but add less
- Self-citations inflate metrics â€” check who's actually citing
- Recent citations more meaningful for living research â€” old papers cited by habit

## Alerts and Tracking
- Create alerts for search terms â€” email when new papers match
- Follow researchers via profiles â€” notifications for new publications
- "Cite" button exports to reference managers â€” BibTeX, EndNote, RefMan
- Save to library requires Google account â€” organizes papers for later

## Limitations to Know
- Coverage biased toward English and STEM â€” humanities and non-English underrepresented
- No quality filter â€” predatory journals appear alongside legitimate ones
- Older papers may not be indexed â€” pre-digital era incomplete
- Cannot search full text â€” only titles, abstracts, and metadata
- Ranking algorithm opaque â€” not always clear why results ordered as shown

## Evaluating Sources
- Check journal reputation before trusting â€” impact factor as rough guide
- Preprints not peer-reviewed â€” valuable for speed, treat as preliminary
- Conference papers vary by field â€” top-tier in CS, lower status in medicine
- Retracted papers may still appear â€” verify paper status before citing
- Thesis and dissertations are gray literature â€” use cautiously

## Search Strategies
- Start broad, narrow with filters â€” missing papers worse than sorting many
- Combine Scholar with domain databases â€” PubMed for medicine, IEEE for engineering
- Snowball: find one good paper, explore its citations and references
- Check review papers for comprehensive coverage â€” synthesize existing knowledge
- Multiple queries with synonyms â€” terminology varies across disciplines

## Common Mistakes
- Trusting citation count as quality measure
- Ignoring publication date â€” fields evolve, old papers may be outdated
- Stopping at first page of results â€” good papers may be buried
- Not checking for retractions or corrections
- Citing papers based only on abstract â€” always read methodology
- Missing preprint versions that are freely available
