# Visitor Lodging - Seattle

## Best Areas to Stay

### Downtown (Pike Place area)
**Best for**: First-time visitors, business travelers
- Walk to Pike Place, waterfront, major attractions
- Cons: Touristy, some blocks feel empty at night, pricier

**Hotels**:
| Hotel | Price Range | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Four Seasons | $$$$ | Best in class, waterfront |
| Inn at the Market | $$$ | Boutique, overlooks Pike Place |
| Thompson Seattle | $$$ | Rooftop bar, modern |
| Hyatt Regency | $$-$$$ | Solid business option |
| Ace Hotel | $$ | Hip, minimalist |

### Capitol Hill
**Best for**: Walkability, nightlife, younger travelers
- Best urban neighborhood in Seattle
- Restaurants, bars, coffee on every block
- Walk to downtown (15-20 min)

**Hotels**:
| Hotel | Price Range | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Graduate Seattle | $$-$$$ | Boutique, great location |
| Silver Cloud | $$ | Solid mid-range |

**Airbnb**: Many options, often better value than hotels here.

### Belltown
**Best for**: Nightlife, restaurants, younger travelers
- Between downtown and Seattle Center
- More local than downtown, lots of dining

**Hotels**:

## Tips

1. **Price varies wildly** by season - Summer 50%+ higher
2. **Book ahead** for summer weekends and holidays
3. **Airbnb** often better for 3+ nights or groups
4. **Parking** is $40-60/night at hotels - consider skipping car
5. **Downtown = walkable** but can feel dead at night
6. **Capitol Hill = lively** but noisier, especially weekends
7. **Check event calendar** - Conferences spike prices
