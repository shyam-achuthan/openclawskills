# Queen Anne and Magnolia - Seattle

## Lower Queen Anne

### Overview
- Gateway to Seattle Center
- Urban, convenience-focused
- Near Space Needle, MoPOP, KeyArena
- More urban than Upper Queen Anne

### Character
- Dense apartments
- Sports/concert crowd on event nights
- Mix of residents and visitors
- Good restaurants, bars

### Living Here

| Aspect | Rating | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Walkability | 85 | Very walkable |
| Transit | Excellent | Buses, near monorail |
| Nightlife | Good | Event-driven |
| Dining | Good | Variety |
| Grocery | Good | Metropolitan Market |
| Safety | Good | Generally safe |
| Rent (1BR) | $1,900-2,500 | Moderate-high |

### Who Lives Here
- Young professionals
- Empty nesters
- People who love Seattle Center events
- Those wanting urban without downtown

### Pros
- Walk to Seattle Center
- Good restaurants
- Not as chaotic as Belltown
- Close to Fremont, Ballard

### Cons
- Event crowds (concerts, sports)
- Less character than other neighborhoods
- Hills to climb to Upper QA
- Some older apartments

### Key Spots
- Dick's Drive-In (burgers)
- Ten Mercer (neighborhood spot)
- Toulouse Petit (brunch)
- Metropolitan Market (fancy grocery)

## Upper Queen Anne

### Overview
- Hilltop residential neighborhood
- Amazing views of city and mountains
- Kerry Park = THE skyline viewpoint
- Quiet, family-friendly

### Character
- Classic Seattle homes
- Tree-lined streets
- Views everywhere
- Small commercial strip (Queen Anne Ave)

### Living Here

| Aspect | Rating | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Walkability | 60 | Commercial strip walkable |
| Transit | Moderate | Buses, but car helpful |
| Nightlife | Limited | Go elsewhere |
| Dining | Good | On Queen Anne Ave |
| Grocery | Good | QFC, Metropolitan |
| Safety | Excellent | Very safe |
| Rent (1BR) | $1,800-2,400 | Moderate |

### Who Lives Here
- Families
- Older professionals
- People who want views
- Those who like quiet

### Pros
- Best views in Seattle (Kerry Park)
- Safe, quiet, residential
- Good restaurants on main strip
- Quick drive to downtown

### Cons
- Hilly (very)
- Car needed for most trips
- Limited nightlife
- Can feel isolated

### Key Spots
- Kerry Park (MUST VISIT - best skyline view)
- Marshall Park (another viewpoint)
- Betty (brunch)
- Canlis (legendary fine dining, splurge)
- How to Cook a Wolf (Tom Douglas)

## Magnolia

### Overview
- Peninsula neighborhood
- Most isolated urban neighborhood
- Discovery Park is here
- Very quiet, residential

### Character
- Suburban feel in city
- Families predominate
- One access bridge (Magnolia Bridge)
- Self-contained community

### Living Here

| Aspect | Rating | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Walkability | 40 | Magnolia Village is walkable |
| Transit | Poor | Buses, but car needed |
| Nightlife | None | Leave neighborhood |
| Dining | Limited | Village has basics |
| Grocery | Limited | One grocery store |
| Safety | Excellent | Very safe |
| Rent (1BR) | $1,700-2,300 | Lower than Queen Anne |

### Who Lives Here
- Families (primarily)
- Retirees
- People who want nature access
- Those fleeing urban density

### Pros
- Discovery Park (Seattle's best park)
- Very quiet and safe
- Nature everywhere
- More affordable than similar areas
- Strong community

### Cons
- Isolated (one bridge access)
- Car required
- Nothing to do at night
- Far from urban amenities
- Can feel suburban

### Discovery Park
- 534 acres of urban wilderness
- Beach access
- Lighthouse
- Hiking trails
- THE reason to live here

### Magnolia Village
- Small commercial strip
- Coffee shops
- Restaurants
- Basic needs

### Best For
- Families with young kids
- Nature lovers
- People who don't need nightlife
- Those with reliable car

## Queen Anne Summary

| Sub-area | Best For | Avoid If |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Lower Queen Anne | Convenience, events | Hate crowds on event nights |
| Upper Queen Anne | Views, quiet, families | Need walkability |
| Magnolia | Nature, families, quiet | Need urban life |
